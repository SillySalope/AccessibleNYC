NYC Public Transit is essential, but if you use a wheelchair, it may be out of reach!

In 2016, 57% of New Yorkers used Public Transit to commute to work1

with a daily average of over 5 million trips on the subway. 2

However, 10% of NYC's population has some disability, and 13% of the population is above the age of 65.

Only 25% of the subway stations are accessible to wheelchairs.

Even though 82% of the NYC population is 1/2 mile from a subway station,1
the vast majority of the subway is not suited for senior and disabled population.2

Services like buses and Access-A-Ride can help these citizens get around NYC,
but they are unreliable and slow.3 4
Thus, NYC Public Transit continues to be a challenge for millions of citizens.
The list below provides some resources for better planning your trip and booking Access-A-Ride,
but until more subway stations are adapted, there is a long way to a more inclusive Public Transit.
